Beekeeping Courses

One-day course from £30 per person

Bring your wellies!

Our one-day beekeeping course is an introduction new beekeepers or beekeepers with less than 2 years’ experience. The morning is a classroom lecture, and the afternoon is a practical demonstration within our hives to allow you "hands on" experience. We will cover all aspects of what you should be looking for within the hives and a inspection so that you understand the life cycle and learn to “read” your bees. Protective clothing will be supplied if required but please bring stout boots or "wellies” and a packed lunch. We run several classes and can arrange one-to-one, family and groups packages.

We offer a discount for larger groups and families.

Honey v Sugar

Honey is actually sweeter than sugar and has more calories per gram than sugar, this does not mean it is worse for you. Honey has always been known for it's health benefits and a recent study showed that honey is better than most over the counter medicines for curing coughs and colds. Sugar is associated with increased inflammation. Honey also contains more vitamins and minerals than sugar.

Find out more at www.healthline.com

Bee-friendly Gardening

Plant wildflowers in your garden to help the bees. I you can sow seeds in early spring it will encourage early germination. Some of the best flowers for bees are Poppy, Musk Mallow, Cornflower, Foxglove, Marigolds, all of these flowers are easy to grow and really help the bees, pollinators and of course the environment.
